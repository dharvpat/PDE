## Core Stochastic Volatility Models

### Heston Model

1. **Heston, S.L. (1993). "A closed-form solution for options with stochastic volatility with applications to bond and currency options."**
   - *Review of Financial Studies*, 6(2), 327-343.
   - Link: https://www.ma.imperial.ac.uk/~ajacquie/IC_Num_Methods/IC_Num_Methods_Docs/Literature/Heston.pdf
   - DOI: 10.1093/rfs/6.2.327
   - **Summary:** Seminal paper introducing the Heston stochastic volatility model with closed-form characteristic function for option pricing.

### SABR Model

2. **Hagan, P.S., Kumar, D., Lesniewski, A.S., & Woodward, D.E. (2002). "Managing smile risk."**
   - *Wilmott Magazine*, September 2002, 84-108.
   - Link: Available through Wilmott Magazine archives and SSRN
   - **Summary:** Introduces the SABR (Stochastic Alpha Beta Rho) model for volatility smile modeling, widely used in interest rate derivatives.

## Optimal Stopping & Mean Reversion

### Optimal Mean-Reversion Trading

3. **Leung, T., & Li, X. (2015). "Optimal Mean Reversion Trading with Transaction Costs and Stop-Loss Exit."**
   - *Journal of Industrial and Management Optimization*
   - Link: https://arxiv.org/pdf/1411.5062
   - arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/1411.5062
   - **Summary:** Rigorous framework for optimal entry/exit timing in mean-reverting spreads using double stopping theory under Ornstein-Uhlenbeck dynamics.

4. **Bertram, W.K. (2010). "Analytic solutions for optimal statistical arbitrage trading."**
   - *Physica A: Statistical Mechanics and its Applications*, 389(11), 2234-2243.
   - DOI: 10.1016/j.physa.2010.01.045
   - **Summary:** Derives analytic formulas for first-passage times and optimal trading thresholds in exponential Ornstein-Uhlenbeck processes.

5. **Leung, T., & Li, X. (2016). "Optimal Mean reversion Trading: Mathematical Analysis and Practical Applications."**
   - Book: World Scientific Publishing
   - Link: https://www.worldscientific.com/worldscibooks/10.1142/9781786341969
   - **Summary:** Comprehensive treatment of optimal stopping problems for statistical arbitrage with detailed implementations.

## Factor Investing & Asset Pricing

### Momentum

6. **Jegadeesh, N., & Titman, S. (1993). "Returns to Buying Winners and Selling Losers: Implications for Stock Market Efficiency."**
   - *Journal of Finance*, 48(1), 65-91.
   - Link: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=1919226
   - DOI: 10.1111/j.1540-6261.1993.tb04702.x
   - **Summary:** Foundational paper documenting the momentum anomaly - past winners continue outperforming losers over 3-12 month horizons.

7. **Jegadeesh, N., & Titman, S. (2011). "Momentum."**
   - *Annual Review of Financial Economics*, 3, 493-509.
   - Link: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=1919226
   - **Summary:** Review article surveying 30 years of momentum research, explanations, and international evidence.

8. **Jegadeesh, N., & Titman, S. (2023). "Momentum: Evidence and Insights 30 Years Later."**
   - *Pacific-Basin Finance Journal*
   - Link: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4602426
   - DOI: 10.1016/j.pacfin.2023.102127
   - **Summary:** Updated evidence on momentum performance in post-2000 period across markets.

9. **Moskowitz, T.J., Ooi, Y.H., & Pedersen, L.H. (2012). "Time series momentum."**
   - *Journal of Financial Economics*, 104(2), 228-250.
   - DOI: 10.1016/j.jfineco.2011.11.003
   - **Summary:** Documents time-series momentum (absolute returns predict future returns) across 58 asset classes globally.

### Value & Multi-Factor Models

10. **Fama, E.F., & French, K.R. (1992). "The Cross-Section of Expected Stock Returns."**
    - *Journal of Finance*, 47(2), 427-465.
    - DOI: 10.1111/j.1540-6261.1992.tb04398.x
    - **Summary:** Introduces size and value factors, showing beta has little power to explain cross-section of returns.

11. **Fama, E.F., & French, K.R. (1993). "Common risk factors in the returns on stocks and bonds."**
    - *Journal of Financial Economics*, 33(1), 3-56.
    - DOI: 10.1016/0304-405X(93)90023-5
    - **Summary:** Presents the famous Fama-French three-factor model (Market, SMB, HML).

12. **Fama, E.F., & French, K.R. (2015). "A Five-Factor Asset Pricing Model."**
    - *Journal of Financial Economics*, 116(1), 1-22.
    - Link: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2287202
    - DOI: 10.1016/j.jfineco.2014.10.010
    - **Summary:** Extends three-factor model by adding profitability (RMW) and investment (CMA) factors; shows HML becomes redundant.

### Low Volatility / Betting Against Beta

13. **Frazzini, A., & Pedersen, L.H. (2014). "Betting Against Beta."**
    - *Journal of Financial Economics*, 111(1), 1-25.
    - Link: https://www.aqr.com/Insights/Research/Journal-Article/Betting-Against-Beta
    - DOI: 10.1016/j.jfineco.2013.10.005
    - **Summary:** Documents low-beta anomaly and proposes BAB factor; argues leverage constraints cause high-beta stocks to be overpriced.

14. **Ang, A., Hodrick, R.J., Xing, Y., & Zhang, X. (2006). "The Cross-Section of Volatility and Expected Returns."**
    - *Journal of Finance*, 61(1), 259-299.
    - DOI: 10.1111/j.1540-6261.2006.00836.x
    - **Summary:** Shows stocks with high idiosyncratic volatility have low average returns - the volatility anomaly.

15. **Barroso, P., Detzel, A., & Maio, P.F. (2025). "The Volatility Puzzle of the Beta Anomaly."**
    - *Journal of Financial Economics*, 165
    - Link: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3882108
    - **Summary:** Shows BAB factor performance varies dramatically with market volatility conditions; institutional demand dynamics explain this.

## Volatility-Managed Portfolios

16. **Moreira, A., & Muir, T. (2017). "Volatility-managed portfolios."**
    - *Journal of Finance*, 72(4), 1611-1644.
    - DOI: 10.1111/jofi.12513
    - **Summary:** Shows that scaling portfolio weights inversely with recent realized volatility significantly improves Sharpe ratios across diverse strategies.

17. **Fleming, J., Kirby, C., & Ostdiek, B. (2001). "The economic value of volatility timing."**
    - *Journal of Finance*, 56(1), 329-352.
    - DOI: 10.1111/0022-1082.00327
    - **Summary:** Early evidence on benefits of volatility timing for portfolio allocation decisions.

## Statistical Arbitrage & Pairs Trading

### Cointegration-Based Pairs Trading

18. **Gatev, E., Goetzmann, W.N., & Rouwenhorst, K.G. (2006). "Pairs trading: Performance of a relative-value arbitrage rule."**
    - *Review of Financial Studies*, 19(3), 797-827.
    - DOI: 10.1093/rfs/hhj020
    - **Summary:** Seminal empirical paper on pairs trading using distance method; documents consistent profits from 1962-2002.

19. **Vidyamurthy, G. (2004). "Pairs Trading: Quantitative Methods and Analysis."**
    - Book: Wiley Finance
    - ISBN: 978-0471460671
    - **Summary:** Comprehensive practitioner guide to pairs trading with focus on cointegration methodology.

20. **Caldeira, J., & Moura, G.V. (2013). "Selection of a Portfolio of Pairs Based on Cointegration: A Statistical Arbitrage Strategy."**
    - *Brazilian Review of Finance*, 11(1), 49-80.
    - Link: https://www.redalyc.org/pdf/3058/305827591004.pdf
    - **Summary:** Portfolio approach to pairs trading; achieved 189% cumulative profit over 4 years with 16.38% annual return.

21. **Avellaneda, M., & Lee, J.-H. (2010). "Statistical arbitrage in the U.S. equities market."**
    - *Quantitative Finance*, 10(7), 761-782.
    - DOI: 10.1080/14697680903124632
    - **Summary:** Mean-reversion framework for statistical arbitrage using principal components analysis.

### Cointegration Theory

22. **Engle, R.F., & Granger, C.W.J. (1987). "Co-integration and error correction: Representation, estimation, and testing."**
    - *Econometrica*, 55(2), 251-276.
    - DOI: 10.2307/1913236
    - **Summary:** Nobel Prize-winning work introducing cointegration theory and error correction models.

## Transaction Costs & Implementation

23. **Frazzini, A., Israel, R., & Moskowitz, T.J. (2012). "Trading costs of asset pricing anomalies."**
    - *Financial Analysts Journal*, 68(2), 103-119.
    - DOI: 10.2469/faj.v68.n2.6
    - **Summary:** Comprehensive study of transaction costs for implementing factor strategies; many anomalies survive realistic costs.

24. **Novy-Marx, R., & Velikov, M. (2016). "A taxonomy of anomalies and their trading costs."**
    - *Review of Financial Studies*, 29(1), 104-147.
    - DOI: 10.1093/rfs/hhv063
    - **Summary:** Analyzes profitability of 23 anomalies after accounting for implementation costs; develops improved cost models.

## Advanced Volatility Modeling

### Volatility Surface Construction

25. **Gatheral, J., & Jacquier, A. (2014). "Arbitrage-free SVI volatility surfaces."**
    - *Quantitative Finance*, 14(1), 59-71.
    - DOI: 10.1080/14697688.2013.819986
    - **Summary:** SVI (Stochastic Volatility Inspired) parameterization for arbitrage-free volatility surfaces.

26. **Cont, R., & Tankov, P. (2004). "Financial Modelling with Jump Processes."**
    - Book: Chapman & Hall/CRC Financial Mathematics Series
    - ISBN: 978-1584884132
    - **Summary:** Comprehensive treatment of jump-diffusion and Lévy process models for asset prices and volatility.

### Local Volatility & PDE Methods

27. **Dupire, B. (1994). "Pricing with a smile."**
    - *Risk Magazine*, 7(1), 18-20.
    - **Summary:** Introduces local volatility model and Dupire formula for recovering local volatility from option prices.

28. **Andersen, L., & Brotherton-Ratcliffe, R. (2005). "Extended LIBOR market models with stochastic volatility."**
    - *Journal of Computational Finance*, 9(1), 1-40.
    - **Summary:** Extensions of LIBOR market model incorporating stochastic volatility for interest rate derivatives.

## Numerical Methods & Calibration

29. **Carr, P., & Madan, D. (1999). "Option valuation using the fast Fourier transform."**
    - *Journal of Computational Finance*, 2(4), 61-73.
    - DOI: 10.21314/JCF.1999.043
    - **Summary:** FFT-based method for efficient option pricing using characteristic functions; widely used for Heston model.

30. **Andersen, L. (2008). "Simple and efficient simulation of the Heston stochastic volatility model."**
    - *Journal of Computational Finance*, 11(3), 1-42.
    - DOI: 10.21314/JCF.2008.189
    - **Summary:** Quadratic-exponential (QE) scheme for accurate Monte Carlo simulation of Heston model.

31. **Le Floc'h, F. (2013). "Fourier Integration and Stochastic Volatility Calibration."**
    - Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1308.3946
    - **Summary:** Efficient adaptive Filon quadrature for Heston option pricing via characteristic function.

## Specific Research on Our Strategies

### Ornstein-Uhlenbeck Optimal Stopping

38. **López, O., & Serrano, R. (2020). "A Closed-form Solution for Optimal Mean-reverting Trading Strategies."**
    - Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.10502
    - **Summary:** Derives closed-form solutions for optimal trading levels by solving Fredholm integral equation; includes profit-taking and stop-loss optimization.

39. **Zeng, Z., & Lee, C.G. (2014). "Pairs trading: optimal thresholds and profitability."**
    - *Quantitative Finance*, 14(11), 1881-1893.
    - DOI: 10.1080/14697688.2014.917806
    - **Summary:** Extension of Bertram (2010) allowing both long and short positions; numerical optimization of thresholds.

### SABR Calibration

40. **Doust, P. (2012). "No-arbitrage SABR."**
    - *Journal of Computational Finance*, 15(3), 3-31.
    - DOI: 10.21314/JCF.2012.247
    - **Summary:** PDE approach to ensure SABR model produces arbitrage-free prices for extreme strikes.

41. **Antonov, A., Konikov, M., & Spector, M. (2015). "The Free Boundary SABR: Natural Extension to Negative Rates."**
    - *Risk Magazine*, April 2015
    - Link: https://www.risk.net/derivatives/2395263/free-boundary-sabr-natural-extension-negative-rates
    - **Summary:** Extension of SABR to handle negative interest rates (shifted SABR).

## How to Access Papers

### Free Access:
1. **arXiv.org** - Preprints freely available
2. **SSRN** - Working papers, often free
3. **Google Scholar** - Search for PDF versions
4. **Author websites** - Many authors post PDFs on personal pages
5. **ResearchGate** - Request full text from authors

### Institutional Access:
- University library subscriptions (JSTOR, ScienceDirect, Wiley)
- Bloomberg Terminal (some papers integrated)

### Purchase:
- Individual articles can be purchased from publishers (~$30-50)
- Consider institutional repository access if conducting serious research
